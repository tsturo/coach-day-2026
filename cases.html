Case A Coach Day · AI — Expectations vs. Reality
A
The senior who became a proofreader.
The situation
  • A senior developer stopped writing code.
  • Their days are spent reviewing AI-generated PRs — often 100+ files each.
  • A mid-level on the same team is thriving — shipping fast, running agents.
  • The senior is quietly updating their CV.
"I used to build things. Now I check if a machine built them correctly. I could be anyone."
Prompts to think with
Use during Task 1 & Task 2
  • What did the senior used to get from their work that they're not getting now?
  • Is the mid-level thriving because of skill, temperament, or career stage?
  • Who else on the team might be quietly feeling what the senior feels?
  • If you split review work evenly, does the problem get smaller — or just more distributed?
  • What would the senior need to hear from you to not send that CV out?
  • What's the team losing that nobody has named out loud yet?
Review overload Motivation Team asymmetry

Case B Coach Day · AI — Expectations vs. Reality
B
The PO who became a developer.
The situation
  • The Product Owner built a working prototype over the weekend with AI.
  • Monday: 2,000 lines of generated code, submitted as a PR to the main repo. "Just review and merge it — it works."
  • The tech lead pushed back on architecture and security.
  • The PO escalated to their manager. Both sides are looking at you.
"The development team is blocking innovation and slowing us down."
Prompts to think with
Use during Task 1 & Task 2
  • What did the PO actually see in their weekend work that made them think they'd "solved" dev work?
  • Where did the PO's ownership end before AI — where does it end now?
  • Is there a version where the prototype was genuinely useful as a starting point?
  • What does the tech lead need to push back without looking obstructionist?
  • If you took the PO's side, what would that do to the tech lead's authority next time?
  • Who decides where the line is — and have they ever written it down?
Role boundaries Architecture bypass Power dynamics

Case C Coach Day · AI — Expectations vs. Reality
C
The team that ran out of work.
The situation
  • Throughput tripled. A 6-month backlog is nearly empty at month 2.
  • The partner is delighted but hasn't prepared more requirements.
  • Two people asked you privately: "Will they still need all of us?" You don't have an honest answer.
  • A third stopped using AI entirely — "why would I help make myself redundant faster?"
Prompts to think with
Use during Task 1 & Task 2
  • Who should you talk to first — the two who asked, or the one who stopped using AI?
  • What does the partner's planning process need to change for this not to repeat?
  • What's the honest version of the answer the two are asking for?
  • If throughput dropped back tomorrow, would anyone feel safer? Why or why not?
  • Who inside Visma needs to hear that "too fast" is a real problem?
  • What would you have to commit to publicly for the AI-refuser to trust the team's direction?

Case D Coach Day · AI — Expectations vs. Reality
D
The silent room.
The situation
  • In a retrospective, a developer says what many have been thinking: nobody has explained why the team is being pushed to adopt AI.
  • They ask directly: is this about impressing Visma? About headcount? They say they'd respect any honest answer.
  • The room goes silent. Everyone turns to you.
  • You can't pass this up the chain in the moment. You also don't actually know the real "why" yourself.
"I'd respect any answer — I just want the real one."
Prompts to think with
Use during Task 1 & Task 2
  • What honest answer could you give in the moment that isn't a lie but isn't the full truth either?
  • What would change if you said: "I don't know — let's find out together"?
  • Who in leadership actually knows the real "why"?
  • Which of your own fears are you protecting by not asking leadership this?
  • What's the cost of silence here — to this developer, and to everyone watching?
  • If leadership won't give a "why", what smaller "why" can you construct from what you do know?

Case E Coach Day · AI — Expectations vs. Reality
E
The bug that slipped through.
The situation
  • Dev throughput doubled. QA capacity didn't.
  • A security vulnerability was caught by the Bug Bounty program — in AI-generated code that had passed review.
  • The reviewer admitted they didn't fully understand that section — but "it looked clean."
  • Team lead wants to slow down. The partner wants to speed up. Trust is shaken on both sides.
"We're not actually going faster. We're finding out about problems later."
Prompts to think with
Use during Task 1 & Task 2
  • What did the reviewer actually believe when they said "it looked clean"?
  • How do you build review culture where "I don't fully understand this" is safe to say?
  • What's the cost of slowing down — vs. the cost of this happening again?
  • Who outside the team (security, partner, Bug Bounty program) needs to be part of the fix?
  • Is the partner's "speed up" a demand, or a hope — and does that change your answer?
  • What would it take for the reviewer to come to you before the next one slips?

Case F Coach Day · AI — Expectations vs. Reality
F
The success stories that won't stop.
The situation
  • Your team is doing fine. Delivery is steady, quality is holding, people are learning AI tools at a healthy pace.
  • But managers — yours and the partner's — keep hearing polished AI success stories: internal demos, LinkedIn posts, "team X shipped in a weekend."
  • Every week you get the same question, in new words: "Why aren't we building like that?"
  • You know most of those stories are survivorship-biased, cherry-picked, or quietly unfinished — but that's a hard argument to win against a highlight reel.
  • Your team is feeling the pressure. Some are cutting corners to "look faster." Others are disengaging.
Prompts to think with
Use during Task 1 & Task 2
  • Which of the success stories have you actually verified? Which are highlight reels?
  • What's the cost of "looking faster" vs. being healthy — can you name it in numbers?
  • What would you need to show your manager to shift the conversation from "why not us" to "what's actually working"?
  • Is there a part of you also quietly believing the highlight reel?
  • What genuine AI wins from your team are going unnoticed because they don't look dramatic?
  • Who else in the company is caught in the same pressure — and could you compare notes?
Benchmark pressure Survivorship bias Protecting the team
